My Devices

Designing an accessible app to enhance the independence of users borrowing assistance devices

Challenge

How might we create a digital solution that supports users of assistance devices to live independently, and how might we include them in the process of receiving these aids?

My role

UX research, Ideation, Prototyping, User testing

Project duration

5 weeks

Software

Figma, Figjam

Introduction

This project sprung from an open brief from a school project: Use design thinking to design something that solves a real problem. In a group of 3 and with a world of possibilities, we found something we were truly interested in solving. One of the group members works as a nurse, and has heard from patients how difficult it is to get hold of assistance devices from Hjelpemiddelsentralen.

The current process is complex and digitally outdated

Hjelpemiddeldatabasen.no is a database of all available assistance devices, where users can read about them. The website is packed with categories and lots of information.

NAV has several websites, and we saw that the process of getting information about assistance devices to have them delivered and maintained in their daily lives was not easy or streamlined.

Norway's approaching elderly wave and independence

The Norwegian government and healthcare section is looking for ways to promote independence for the population by helping them to live in their homes for as long as possible.

The solution we created seeks to alleviate some of the strain on Norway's healthcare system by fostering a cohesive and cooperative way to manage assistance devices. The current process makes users who need assistance devices heavily depend on relatives to get hold of these, making users less independent in their own lives. Some users are dependent on caregivers, and the process should make it easier to include them.

Learning more about the problem

Following the design thinking methodology, we created research goals to guide our process into learning more about our potential users.

Literature reviews

15-18% of Norway's population has a disability, and 440 613 people used at least one technical aid as of 2022.

Competitor analysis

As there are no direct competitors of the digital solution we were creating, we chose three digital services that provided information about assistance devices or renting items online.

This includes a visually appealing design, a safe way to log in, easy ways to contact support, and provide status updates for users to stay in control

User interviews

We had to go through two rounds of user interviews, as we identified a different problem area than we first set out to research during the first round.

We were more focused on the delivery of assistance devices, but it seemed that other problems were more prevalent: Users need easier access to information.

Both research rounds were analyzed separately using affinity mapping, and provided us with valuable insights we brought with us throughout the project:

  1. Users are dependent on healthcare professionals or relatives when applying for and receiving assistance devices, as the process is difficult to complete alone
  2. Users needs to have support easily available in case of questions, inquiries or problems
  3. Users wants updates throughout the process of applying and receiving
  4. Making information easily available makes the users better equipped to live independently, and will help relieve pressure on healthcare and municipality workers

Before creating a final solution, we went through the iterative process of creating wireframes and testing them, improving the wireframes and testing again. Feedback from the user testing rounds gave us valuable insights:

Some parts of the app were difficult to navigate, mostly because of the name and icon. However, information on other pages was presented intuitively and simply, making it easy for them to complete the task we gave them. We also learned that users appreciate having multiple options to find the information they need, so they can use the option they prefer. For instance, a quick photo guide instead of reading a manual to learn how to use the assistance device.

We did not have the opportunity to test with users with assistance devices

For this project, we struggled with recruiting actual users of assistance devices. We lack feedback from and testing with them that would help us see if our solution is actually acessible and helpful. We did the best we could with the time and resources we had for the project duration, and managed to gather some insight from people who have used Hjelpemiddelsentralen for relatives, or people who have worked with users of assistance devices.

We also used our knowledge of accessibility guidelines such as WCAG, to ensure better accessibility as our user group mainly consists of elderly people and people who use assistance devices that might need more help with digital solutions

Our goal was to help users with assistance devices, as well as help relieve anyone who works with them by encouraging independence. This could potentially play a part in preparing Norway for the anticipated wave of the elderly population.

Results

Our solution was created from the research we did, our ideation and feedback from user testing. We explored how we can help users with assistance devices be independent, and how we can help them and/or their caregivers manage the process from start to finish. The goal was to help them stay updated about applications, deliveries, information about devices and how to reach out for support.
